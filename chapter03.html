Chapter3

The Secret Corridor

"It can be a coincidence," he cried, at last springing from his chair and pacing wildly up and down the room; "It is impossible that it should be a mere coincidence. What can it mean? Surely my whole chain of reasoning cannot have been false. It is impossible! And yet this wretched stone is none the worse. Ah, I have it! I have it!" With a perfect shriek of delight he rushed to the box, looked at the stone. Sherlock Holmes drew a long breath, and wiped the perspiration from his forehand. "I should have more faith", he said; "I ought to know by this time that when a fact appears to be opposed to a long train of deductions, it invariably proves to be capable of bearing other interpretation. I ought to have known that before ever I saw the stone at all". This last statement appeared to me to be so startling, that I could hardly believe that he was in his sober senses. There was the stone, however, to prove that his conjecture had been correct. It seemed to me that the mists in my own mind were gradually clearing away, and I began to have a dim, vague perception of the truth.

Holmes had called Lestrade and the three of us went to Lord Adler's house in a cab. Holmes had everything ready for his final act. The one that will finally uncover the truth and the one that will give us more information about Lord Adler's whereabouts. Lestrade and I exchanged glances and when we arrived, Holmes led us upstairs. But this time not to Lord Adler's room, but to Miss Adler's. Miss Adler's room was bright and tidy, however it gave me the creeps. There was something, more like a cold welcoming, a cold... In that moment, Holmes broke the wall with his foot and a dark passage appeared in front of our eyes.

I hesitated for a moment, but Holmes had long entered the passage with Lestrade and both had dissapeared from my sight. I took a lamp and followed them. The passage was made of large stones, dark stones built the walls and the steps lead the way down. There was a smell of something I could not recognized, but it was unpleasant and I was starting to feeling sick. I heard a gun shoot and quick steps were coming into my direction. I raised the lamp with my left hand, I took my pistol and pointed at the dark. "Holmes, Lestrade" I yelled "Who is there? What is happening?". I could hear the steps getting closer and there was no answer from Holmes yet.

"Stop! or I'll shoot" I said. For a moment I thought about all possible scenarios. So, I run toward the entrance of the passage, It was better to face whoever was running in my direction from there. I got to the room and locked the door. I pulled some furniture as barricade and wait pointing my pistol at the passage. I could not believe what I saw a few minutes later. "It is Lord Adler!" I thought, but he seemed like a beast, his skin had turned blue and he was very agitated. He was naked and he was wearing only a pair of boots, when he saw me he tried to escape. I knew for his blue skin that he had argyria, as a result of probably working with silver. But I had never seen something like that. His violent state suggested as well that he was having some kind of psychotic episode. Holmes and Lestrade came out from the passage and held him.

Lord Adler died two days after we found him. The poisoning with silver and other heavy metal caused him a painful death. We attended his funeral and we were summon to appear before the magistrates upon the same day regarding the gardener's death. What we found down the passage will leave a deep trace in my memory. The passage when down about 100 meters and ended in a big illuminated room. Nothing there seemed ordinary, four big tables where located beside the wall, in the centre a big pot was still warm. Over the table we found heavy metals like lead, silver and aluminium, and lab equipment for an alchemist.

However, the different experiments that Lord Adler was performing not only involved metals, but human flesh. We found on the corner a cage with two little children dead for no more than a week, as well as three human hearts.Lestrade found a pile of documents that implicated a more than a dozen of people only in London and almost a hundred in all England. It seems that Lord Adler sold the idea of having found the philosophical stone. Upon the promise of 'the elixir of life', he got all kind of elements for his experiments, even human beings.

Holmes explain to Miss Adler and Lestrade how he discover everything. "The first clue was the blood on the wall, I knew from the moment I saw it that it had traces of silver. Then, after analysing the box and the gardener's piece of paper, I came to the conclusion that so many metals were only possible if Lord Adler had worked in a mine or in an alchemist lab." said Holmes. "But how did you know where the lab was?" asked Miss Adler. "When I broke the wall to get Lord Adler's secret box, I noticed a current of air coming from the wall." said Holmes. The rest of the story is well known for all of you. "Now, Lestrade, is time for you to do your job and trace this mad business. Doctor Watson, we have an appointment. Our next case is awaiting us" indicated Holmes leaving the room.

THE END
